# EMARX Paragraph Moves Protocol

This protocol is derived from the user's workspace corpus. It does not add new prohibitions; it describes how mature Chinese academic papers in this field actually move from sentence to sentence and from paragraph to paragraph.

## What the Corpus Shows

Papers in the workspace that succeed as 学理论文 do not put a case in every paragraph. Instead, they alternate between a small set of recognizable paragraph functions. A paragraph is evaluated by whether it performs its function well, not by whether it contains a case.

The most common paragraph functions are:

1. **Topic-setting paragraph** — states the analytical problem or the section's central claim.
2. **Mechanism paragraph** — explains how a process works, what turns A into B.
3. **Evidence paragraph** — brings in a source, case, data, policy text, or empirical finding.
4. **Boundary paragraph** — distinguishes concepts, limits scope, or clarifies definitions.
5. **Transition paragraph** — carries the argument from one move to the next.
6. **Judgment paragraph** — lands a claim and states what it means for the thesis.

A well-written section usually contains several of these functions in order, not the same function repeated.

## Typical Section Rhythm

A common rhythm in the corpus is:

```text
topic-setting -> mechanism -> evidence -> judgment -> transition -> next mechanism
```

For example:

- Paragraph 1: states what the section will explain.
- Paragraph 2: explains the mechanism or theoretical relation.
- Paragraph 3: provides a case, data, or source that shows the mechanism at work.
- Paragraph 4: draws the judgment and connects it back to the thesis.

This means a paragraph can be fully conceptual as long as it is followed or preceded by a paragraph that supplies material.

## Paragraph Functions in Detail

### Topic-Setting Paragraph

Job: tell the reader what question this section answers.

Typical opening moves:

- name the object;
- name the theoretical relation;
- name the gap or tension.

Example from corpus style:

> 国际传播的实质是跨文化的沟通交流。人工智能以精准算法和大数据模型为技术依托，实现人机互动基础上的自我学习与自我建构。

This paragraph sets up the object and the mechanism. It does not yet bring a case.

### Mechanism Paragraph

Job: explain the causal or structural process.

Typical opening moves:

- one factor changes another;
- a model, platform, or policy reorganizes relations;
- a theoretical concept makes the process visible.

Example from corpus style:

> 大模型作为元媒介，通过参数设置、训练数据筛选与优化目标定义，重构了信息生产与分发的底层规则。

### Evidence Paragraph

Job: anchor the mechanism in something concrete.

Typical content:

- a named policy document;
- a statistic or market figure;
- a concrete product, platform, or case;
- a quotation or finding from a cited source.

Example from corpus style:

> 2024 年，中国自主研发游戏在海外市场的实际销售收入达到 185.57 亿美元，同比增长 13.39%。《原神》《黑神话：悟空》等产品的海外热度，使数字游戏成为中华优秀传统文化走出去的重要载体。

### Boundary Paragraph

Job: prevent over-generalization by limiting scope.

Typical opening moves:

- distinguish two concepts;
- state the level at which the claim is valid;
- name a condition under which the argument does not hold.

Example from corpus style:

> 表层趋同体现为对目标受众熟悉的传播平台、流行语态及视觉符号的采纳。这种调适是必要的，它降低了文化内容的接触门槛。然而，若仅止步于此，中华文化国际传播容易沦为对表层文化符号的机械表达。

### Transition Paragraph

Job: connect two analytical moves.

The corpus uses transitions naturally. Words such as `在此基础上`, `由此`, `因此`, `然而`, `与此同时`, `另一方面`, `进一步看`, `换言之`, `这意味着` are common and acceptable when they name a real movement.

Weak use is when the transition word is the only connective tissue and the reasoning has not moved. Strong use is when the transition sentence explains what has changed between the previous claim and the next claim.

### Judgment Paragraph

Job: state what the analysis proves and return to the thesis.

Typical closing moves:

- summarize the section's finding;
- state the limit of the finding;
- point to the next section.

## Sequence Words Are Allowed When They Organize Real Content

The corpus regularly uses:

- `一方面...另一方面...`
- `首先...其次...再次...最后...`
- `第一...第二...第三...`

These are not automatically mechanical. They become mechanical only when:

- they replace reasoning with listing;
- the items are parallel slogans rather than analytical steps;
- the sequence does not build toward a judgment.

When the items genuinely unpack a mechanism or compare distinct dimensions, sequence words are appropriate.

## What to Avoid

- a section made entirely of topic-setting paragraphs;
- a section made entirely of evidence paragraphs with no mechanism or judgment;
- repeating the same paragraph function three or more times in a row;
- using a transition word when the reasoning has not moved;
- ending a section with a list instead of a judgment.

## Practical Check

Before finalizing a section, label each paragraph with its function:

```text
P1: topic-setting
P2: mechanism
P3: evidence
P4: judgment
```

If the labels are all the same, revise the section. If the section lacks evidence paragraphs, add material. If it lacks mechanism or judgment paragraphs, add reasoning.
